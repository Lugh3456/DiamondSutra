第二十三章 · 净心行善分

金刚般若波罗蜜经

← 第二十二章 目录 Contents 第二十四章 →

「复次，须菩提！是法平等，无有高下，是名阿耨多罗三藐三菩提；以无我、无人、无众生、无寿者，修一切善法，则得阿耨多罗三藐三菩提。须菩提！所言善法者，如来说即非善法，是名善法。」

逐句解释

是法平等，无有高下

这个「法」——无上正等正觉——是平等的，没有高下之分。它不是某些特别的人才能达到的，也不是某些修行方法专属的。它是一切存在的本来性质，对任何人都平等开放，没有任何偏私。「平等」在这里不是道德上的公平，而是本体论上的：实相对所有存在都是同样的实相。

This dharma is level and equal — there is no high or low

Supreme enlightenment is not a peak reached by the few while others remain stuck at the bottom. It is the nature of reality itself — equally present in every being, every moment, every place. There is no enlightenment 'above' and ordinary existence 'below.' The apparent difference between an awakened and an unawakened being is not a difference in the nature of reality available to them — it is a difference in what they are doing with it. One is open to it; the other is temporarily obscuring it. The ground is the same.

以无我、无人、无众生、无寿者，修一切善法，则得阿耨多罗三藐三菩提

用「无四相」的心去修一切善法——不执着于「我在修行」「有人受益」「有众生得度」——这就是证得无上觉悟的方式。善法不是不要做，而是要以无我的心去做。无我不是不行动，而是行动中没有执着的「我」在把持控制。

Practising all virtuous actions with no sense of self, person, being, or lifespan — this is how supreme enlightenment is attained

After all the deconstruction — no fixed dharma, no beings, no attainment — the sutra now gives a clear, practical answer: do everything good, but do it without ego. The four marks (self, person, being, lifespan) are not a reason to stop acting; they are the framework of identity that, when released, makes action genuinely pure. A doctor who heals without any thought of reputation, payment, or gratitude is still healing — but the quality of the act is completely different from one performed for self-benefit. Ego-free action is the path, not the absence of action.

所言善法者，如来说即非善法，是名善法

就连「善法」这个概念，也是「A即非A」：所谓的善法，不是固定不变的善法，所以叫善法。这提醒修行者：不要把「做善事」变成一个执着的身份认同或道德成就的积累。善是活的、流动的、无我的——一旦执着于「我在行善」，善就已经变质了。

What is called virtuous dharma — the Tathagata says is not virtuous dharma, therefore it is called virtuous dharma

Even virtue gets deconstructed. 'Virtuous practice' is not a fixed category of actions you can collect and store. The moment you begin thinking 'I am a virtuous person' or 'I have accumulated good deeds,' you have turned virtue into an ego-trophy — which immediately corrupts it. True virtue is spontaneous, responsive, and leaves no residue of self-congratulation. It is called virtuous because it functions as virtue in the world — not because it is a permanent quality owned by a permanent self.

总结 · Summary

第二十三章是《金刚经》中最平衡的章节：在所有的「无」和「非」之后，佛陀给出了清晰的实践指引——以无我的心修一切善法。这个「法」对所有人平等，无高无下。善不是不要做，而是要以无我的方式去做。就连「善法」本身，也不是固定的标签，而是活的行动。

Chapter 23 is the sutra's most balanced teaching: after extensive deconstruction, it offers a clear practical instruction — practise all virtuous actions, but do so without any sense of self, other, being, or lifespan. The dharma of awakening is level and available to all equally. Virtue is not abandoned — it is purified. And even 'virtuous practice' is deconstructed: true virtue is not a trophy but a living, egoless response to what each moment calls for.
